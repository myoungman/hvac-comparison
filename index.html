Key Findings

What we learned through the evaluation

Finding 1 — No contractor mentioned compressor staging
All contractors led with variable-speed blower as the humidity-control story. The variable-speed compressor is actually the more impactful upgrade for summer comfort in Northern Virginia's humid climate. The blower upgrade is real but secondary.
Finding 2 — Universal 80% AFUE across all quotes
Not one of four contractors offered or mentioned a high-efficiency (96%+) furnace. The efficiency difference saves ~$150–250/year in gas. Payback is 9–15 years. Switching to 96%+ requires PVC venting and orphans the water heater on the chimney, requiring a liner.
Finding 3 — No Manual J calculations
Three contractors gave three different furnace BTU answers: 80k, 90k, and 100k. This spread strongly suggests rule-of-thumb sizing rather than load calculations. Your current 100k is likely oversized for the house.
Finding 4 — Reddick's upgraded quote is the standout
The GXV6SS36 is Daikin/Goodman's variable-speed inverter product with a lifetime compressor warranty including full unit replacement — the best warranty in any quote. At ~$15,572 (ex chimney) it undercuts the Carrier Infinity by $2,579 while being quieter in quiet mode.
Finding 5 — Lennox SL280VK is 80% AFUE, not 97%
The "SL" Signature prefix on the Lennox furnace denotes Lennox's premium 80% furnace line — not high-efficiency. The Lennox Elite package (single-stage AC + SL280VK) offers a premium furnace but the weakest AC compressor for its price tier.
Finding 6 — Chimney kit is a wash
Reddick's $840 Chimney Friendly Kit protects masonry chimney liners from 80% furnace condensation. All contractors would need to address this. Excluded from price comparisons as a result.
Finding 7 — Communicating systems require proprietary thermostats
Both variable-speed inverter options (Goodman GXV6SS and Carrier Infinity) require their own communicating thermostats to operate in variable-speed mode. A Nest thermostat works only with single-stage and two-stage systems (Carrier Performance, Trane).
