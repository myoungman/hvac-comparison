Sizing Discrepancy
Three contractors gave three different furnace BTU answers — 80k (Trane), 90k (Carrier), 100k (Reddick). No contractor appears to have run a Manual J load calculation. Ask each for their sizing rationale. For a well-insulated home your size in Falls Church, 80–90k input is likely correct.

🏆 Our Pick · ~5-Year Ownership, Comfort-First
Shanahan Trane Premium (5TTR7) — $14,825 all-in. The 5TTR7 is not a two-stage AC — it's Trane's new multi-speed compressor running up to 6 speeds, explicitly designed as a bridge between two-stage and variable-speed inverter. Combined with the S8V2-CB two-stage furnace and variable-speed blower, you get multi-stage cooling (meaningfully better summer dehumidification than a true two-stage) AND two-stage heating (even, gentle heat cycles) — the full comfort package at a price that makes sense for a 5-year window. At $14,825 it's $1,050 less than the Goodman inverter option and $2,918 less than the Trane Ultimate, without giving up the comfort gains that matter most in Northern Virginia's humid summers. Note: Start by adjusting the manual dampers at the air handler to restrict flow to the hallway and push more toward the upstairs bedroom and office — this addresses the room temperature imbalance before spending anything extra.

Otherwise Trane Ultimate offers the same inverter experience for less.
